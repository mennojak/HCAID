Frequently Asked Questions

How does Artery Alert predict my risk of heart disease?

Artery Alert employs an advanced Decision Tree algorithm that takes into account various aspects of your health data, such as age, gender, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and lifestyle factors. Our model is designed to provide precise predictions, boasting an accuracy score of 84%. To enhance your understanding, we also include a visual representation of the final Decision Tree, allowing you to see how your risk is assessed. The probability of heart disease is determined based on the predictions made by this Decision Tree.

How accurate is Artery Alert in predicting heart disease?

Artery Alert is highly accurate, with an impressive 84% accuracy rate. While it's a valuable tool for assessing your risk, it should not replace professional medical advice. It's designed to complement your health management and keep you informed.

Is my personal and health data safe with Artery Alert?

Absolutely. Artery Alert prioritizes your privacy by not saving your data. We believe in keeping you anonymous, which is even better than just securely storing your information. Your data remains fully anonymous, providing an extra layer of security.

Can Artery Alert replace my regular cardiac check-ups?

No, Artery Alert is not intended to replace professional healthcare consultations. It's a supplementary tool designed to help you monitor and manage your heart health. Regular check-ups with healthcare professionals are essential to accurately assess, diagnose, and manage heart diseases and other health conditions.

How can I interpret the results provided by Artery Alert?

Artery Alert provides a risk assessment along with insightful information about heart health. The results are designed to be user-friendly, offering straightforward insights into your cardiovascular risk profile. Nevertheless, for a thorough understanding and advice on managing your heart health, it's always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional.

Does Artery Alert offer advice on reducing my risk of heart disease?

Yes, Artery Alert provides general advice and tips on managing and potentially reducing your risk of heart disease. These might involve lifestyle changes, dietary advice, and stress management tips. However, for a personalized health plan, it is crucial to consult with healthcare providers who can assess your overall health and create a tailored strategy.
